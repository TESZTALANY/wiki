Treaty of Shimonoseki

The Treaty of Shimonoseki is the treaty marking the resolution of the First Sino-Japanese War. It was signed on April 17, 1895, and ceded the island of Taiwan to the empire of Japan, ending the Qing Chinese Empire's 211 year long reign over it.

Following their gaining jurisdiction over Taiwan, they kept the borders open for two years, which was an ingenious strategy of getting rid of the most hardcore opposition.
