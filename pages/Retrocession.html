Retrocession

Taiwan's Retrocession to China on 25 October, 1945 marks the returning of Taiwan to a Chinese political entity, in this case the Republic of China in 1945.

The Chinese administration with Governor Chen Yi at the helm strongly resembled a colonial one, as Taiwan was considered a conquered territory, whose resources were exploited and looted to help the war effort on the Mainland.

Taiwan was widely seen as different, since it had just spent the past half century on the side of Japan and hadn't experienced the chaos and turmoil of early 20th century China. While the local Taiwanese interpreted these differences as a basis for Taiwan to maintain more autonomy, ROC officials saw them as reason for increased reintegration measures, which was only reinforced by the dissent and unrest on Taiwan, such as the 2-28 Incident.
