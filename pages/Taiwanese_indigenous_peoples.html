Taiwanese indigenous peoples

The indigenous peoples of Taiwan are Austronesian peoples whose ancestors have inhabited the island for approximately 4000 years.1 According to census data, they constitute 2.48% of Taiwan's popuation.2 The Taiwanese government officially recognises 16 indigenous groups and tribes, which are, in descending order by their population sizes: Amis, Paiwan, Atayal, Bunun, Taroko, Puyuma, Rukai, Seediq, Tsou, Saisiyat, Yami, Kavalan, Sakizaya, Thao, Kanakanavu and Saaroa.3
