Zheng Chenggong

Zheng Chenggong, also known by his nickname Koxinga, was a warlord who captured Taiwan from the Dutch in 1662 and founded the Kingdom of Tungtu, later renamed to Kingdom of Tungning by his son.

His father, Zheng Zhilong, was the commander of a pirate fleet patrolling the vicinity of the Taiwan Straight. After having his Taiwanese base captured and himself kidnapped by the Dutch, he joined forces with the Ming Dynasty, which he betrayed during its owerthrowing by the Qing.

Zheng Chenggong, however, remained loyal to the Ming and after his failed assault of the young Qing Dynasty in 1959, he fled to Taiwan in 1661, and expelled the Dutch fully by 1662.

His brief reign over the island was characterised by agricultural developments and early efforts to sinicize Taiwan through the introduction of education and spreading of Confucian values. His son, Zheng Jing, inherited the throne following his early death in 1662. The Qing eventually ended up capturing the island from his grandson in 1683
